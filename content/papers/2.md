##### Abstract

As global temperatures continue to rise due to climate change, heatwaves are becoming more frequent and intense, making it important to examine individual protective strategies against their negative health effects. We investigate the prevalence of eight heat-adaptive behaviors (HABs) and the factors associated with their adoption in the French population. The 1,515 participants were surveyed via an online questionnaire launched in summer 2022. Using an extended version of protection motivation theory (PMT) and a hierarchical analysis, we first examined the HABs in an overall index. Then, we opted to analyze the HABs separately by performing ordered logistic regressions. We found that the population heterogeneously implemented the eight HABs, with “closing the shutters, blinds or curtains” and “going to a cooler or air-conditioned place” being the most and least adopted HAB, respectively. Very good self-reported health is associated with higher adoption of HABs in general. Conversely, greater perception of vulnerability, severity or HABs’ efficacy induces greater heat adaptation, in general. By considering the HABs individually, we obtain more precise results. Two variables are systematically associated with the eight HABs: perceived efficacy, descriptive norms. Response cost is also associated with all but one HABs (reducing the consumption of dehydrating beverages). On the contrary, socioeconomic and demographic characteristics and threat appraisal variables have a heterogeneous impact on the eight HABs. Our study identified important drivers of HAB adoption that might help health authorities to shape their communication campaigns to improve the population's adaptation to heatwaves.
